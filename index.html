Disability Awareness Training

About us
At AbilityWise, we work with schools, community groups and businesses in the public and private sector to provide Disability Awareness Training in an accurate, engaging, and dynamic manner.

AbilityWise also works with authors to provide Disability Sensitivity Reading.

Our Courses are delivered by highly qualified, professional trainers with first-hand experience of having a disability. This allows participants the opportunity to avail of candid discussions and to learn about experiences of living with a disability.

Our highly interactive courses ensure that training is dynamic and fun with diverse pedagogical methods used to create team activities throughout. Scenarios, photographic analysis, simulations, case studies, discussions, and an anonymous question box, ensure that training with AbilityWise caters to a diverse audience to create a comfortable, engaging and stimulating programme that allows attendees to question misconceptions while reflecting and evaluating their own practices of inclusion.
The Importance of Disability Awareness Training
The Central Statistics Office report that 13% of the Irish population have a disability. It is therefore reasonable to assume that most people will have interactions with people who have a disability. Disability Awareness Training has become an essential tool in cultivating an inclusive and accepting environment which is compliant with legislation and provides valuable insight into how to develop your disability inclusive strategy for colleagues, customers, and peers.
Disability Awareness Training in the Workplace and Community Groups
Disability Awareness training improves customer service for clients with disabilities and creates a more diverse and inclusive workplace to support all staff. By identifying misconceptions and promoting a respectful and inclusive environment, employees attending our courses will gain necessary skills and confidence when interacting with colleagues and customers who have a disability. Promoting equality through Disability Awareness Training, eliminates discrimination and creates an open and welcoming environment.

Our courses are compliant for funding from The Department of Social Protection. The Disability Awareness Support Scheme (DASS) provides employers in the private sector with 90% funding towards the cost of Disability Awareness Training in the first year and 80% funding in subsequent years up to a maximum of €20,000.

Our training programmes provide attendees with an understanding and awareness of issues surrounding the disabled community through specific information on:
• relevant anti-discrimination and equality legislation,
• reasonable accommodations,
• government incentives for businesses to hire people with disabilities,
• disability etiquette to clarify appropriate language and behaviour,
• perceptual awareness exercises that aim to dispel common myths,
• disabilities and abilities, focusing on mobility, developmental, sensory issues, and mental health issues.

Bespoke training courses are available following a consultation to target areas of relevance to your business needs.
Schools

Our Disability Awareness course for schools, promotes inclusion, and cultivates an environment of acceptance of diversity, thus preventing or reducing bullying within the school environment.

By identifying and rectifying misconceptions and enhancing a respectful environment, students develop knowledge of significant issues relevant to those within the disabled community and gain confidence in their interactions.

A Universal Design approach to courses at AbilityWise, ensures that diverse learning styles are considered, and a range of pedagogical methods are enforced through our highly interactive course such as through the use of;
• a PowerPoint Presentation,
• photographic analysis,
• simulations,
• videos,
• etiquette awareness discussions and advice,
• interactives games and
• an anonymous question box,

Students learn both independently and within groups in a fun but sensitive manner. As tutors delivering our courses also have an invisible disability, the course is relatable, and students are provided with an opportunity to ask questions directly to someone with a disability. This also ensures the increased comfort of students with disabilities in attendance of the course. Courses are a minimum of 3.5 hours long with extended courses available. Bespoke courses can be tailored to match the needs within your school.

Disability Sensitivity Reading Service
Combining two passions, literature, and disability awareness, AbilityWise provides Disability Sensitivity Reading to authors and playwriters.
Working with Amazon bestselling authors such as Allie Creswell, on her recent dual timeline book, The Cottage on Winter Moss,

Providing a holistic analysis of the authenticity of representation of disabilities in manuscripts, AbilityWise, assesses accuracy and sensitivities to prevent misrepresentation and negative stereotypes through inaccurate portrayals.

AbilityWise’s sensitivity reading service provides advice and guidance to ensure that authentic representations are rendered through assessing language and terminology, stereotypes, story plot and the accuracy of portrayals of characters. Our sensitivity readers have both personal and professional experiences of disabilities.
